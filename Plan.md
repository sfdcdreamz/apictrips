Meeting Purpose
User research on group travel planning pain points.

Key Takeaways
Planning Frustrations: The mentor's main pain points are group indecision over minor details (e.g., hotel ratings) and last-minute plan changes after bookings are made.

Tool Fragmentation: Planning is scattered across disparate tools (WhatsApp, Google Sheets, Splitwise), creating a fragmented workflow.

Budget-First Approach: Destination choice is primarily driven by budget (70–80% of the time), with a strong preference for avoiding peak-season search pricing.

Child-Specific Constraints: Traveling with children requires major adjustments, such as choosing Airbnb for kitchen access and avoiding packed itineraries.

Topics
User Profile & Travel Habits
Frequency: 4–5 international trips/year; ~1 monthly local trip.

Companions:

60% with girlfriend (1–4 people)

30% with friends (6–7 people)

10% with family (7 people)

Budget: 10–15% of annual income.

Satisfaction: ~60–70% of trips are successful; the other 30–40% are frustrating due to people, weather, or location vibe.

Planning & Execution
Planning Styles:

Structured (Girlfriend): Uses a shared Excel sheet for a day-by-day itinerary with columns for individual preferences and booking status.

Improv (Friends): Loose planning via WhatsApp group chats.

Delegated (Parents): Parents decide destination/dates; mentor handles all bookings and logistics.

Decision-Making:

Primary Drivers (80–90%): Aligned dates and cheapest flights/stay.

Secondary Driver (20–30%): Destination desire, where budget is less of a concern.

Research: Relies on Google, YouTube vlogs, and personal recommendations. Not directly influenced by Instagram Reels, but friends are.

Pace: Prefers a "slow travel" philosophy. Itineraries are semi-structured with one main activity per day to ensure flexibility.

Key Pain Points
Group Indecision: Frustration over groups getting stuck on minor details (e.g., hotel ratings) instead of making a decision and moving on.

Last-Minute Changes: Annoyance when plans are altered after bookings are made, especially for non-negotiable activities (e.g., a planned hike in Iceland).

Unreliable Group Members: Disappointment when a trip is canceled or shortened because one person drops out, forcing a reschedule.

Expense Tracking: While Splitwise is the default, logging expenses is imperfect. Unclear bank descriptions can lead to missed entries, which the mentor prefers over logging an expense incorrectly.

Tooling & Platform Usage
Itinerary: Google Sheets, Google Calendar.

Flights: Skyscanner (for browsing), then books directly with the airline.

Accommodation:

Booking.com: Used for hotels despite a disliked UI.

Airbnb: Preferred for its clear UI and kitchen access, especially with children.

Hostelworld: Used for budget-conscious trips.

Local Platforms: Avoided due to buggy interfaces and payment concerns.

Expense Tracking: Splitwise (free version).

Commute: Researches local transport options (public transit, ride-sharing) based on the itinerary and city.

Child-Specific Travel Considerations
Accommodation: Prefers Airbnb for kitchen access to prepare meals for children.

Itinerary: Avoids packed schedules, opting for relaxing destinations to prevent guilt over missed activities.

Logistics: Researches destination terrain to decide on gear (e.g., a carrier over a stroller for rocky areas).

Nanny Services: Trusts hotel-provided nannies but is hesitant to use unknown services found online.
